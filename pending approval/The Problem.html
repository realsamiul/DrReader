The Tk 32,000 Crore Problem: Why Bangladesh's Future is Buried in Paper.

See Our "Two-Bucket" Solution
Bangladesh's economy is bottlenecked by a foundational inefficiency: the manual processing of 256 million documents annually. This wastes over 64 million skilled hours and costs the economy Tk 32,000 Crore every year in lost productivity. This is not a theoretical number; it is a daily, structural weakness in your bank branches and land registries.

64 Million Wasted Hours

Skilled labor, from bank officers to government staff, spends an average of 15 minutes per document on manual entry—a systemic waste of human capital.

10-15% Manual Error Rate

Manual data entry carries a 10-15% error rate, a direct and persistent source of financial loss, compliance failure, and multi-crore fraud vulnerabilities.
